# Delete Your Gospel Vox Account

**Last Updated:** 2 June 2026

This page explains how to delete your Gospel Vox account and what
happens to your data when you do. It is published in compliance with
Google Play's Account Deletion Disclosure requirement and Apple App
Store Guideline 5.1.1(v).

You can delete your account at any time. There is **no waiting
period** and **no contact with support is required**.

---

## In-app deletion (fastest)

1. Open the Gospel Vox app and sign in with the account you want
   to delete.
2. Go to **Settings** → **Account** → **Delete Account**.
3. Confirm the deletion when prompted.

Your account will be marked for deletion immediately. You will be
signed out and unable to sign in again with that identity.

## Web request (if you can't access the app)

If you cannot access the app — for example, if you've changed phones,
lost access to your sign-in provider, or already removed the app —
email **support@gospelvox.com** from the email address linked
to your Gospel Vox account, with:

- **Subject:** "Account Deletion Request"
- **Body:** the phone number and/or email address associated with the
  account.

We will verify ownership of the account (by matching the email or by
asking for a one-time code we send to your registered phone) and
complete the deletion within **30 days** of verification.

## What gets deleted

When you delete your account, the following data is removed or
irreversibly anonymised:

- Your **profile**: name, photo, bio, language and preferences.
- Your **chat history** with Speakers (your side of the conversation).
- Your **reviews and ratings** posted on the Platform.
- Your **matrimony profile** (if you used the matrimony feature).
- Your **push-notification tokens** and device identifiers.
- Your **Wallet balance**: any unused Coins are forfeited at the time
  of deletion.
- Speaker-side: your **profile, bio, photo, languages,
  specialisations**, and **bank/UPI details** beyond the period
  required by tax / KYC law.

## What we are legally required to keep

A small set of records must be retained even after account deletion,
because Indian and international law requires it. We keep these only
for as long as the law requires:

| Record | Why we keep it | Period |
|---|---|---|
| Financial transaction records (Coin purchases, Speaker earnings, withdrawals) | Income Tax Act, 1961 and GST law — bookkeeping obligation | **8 years** from the end of the relevant financial year |
| Speaker KYC documents (ID proof, ministry certificate) | KYC / AML obligations on Speakers who received payouts | **5–8 years** depending on the obligation |
| Records needed to defend, exercise, or establish legal claims | Lawful interest of the operator | Duration of the claim plus limitation period |
| Aggregated, non-identifying analytics | Service improvement; no longer identifies you | Indefinite |

Once these legal retention periods expire, the data is deleted or
irreversibly anonymised.

## Effect on Sessions and Speakers

If you have ongoing Sessions or scheduled Bible Sessions at the time
of deletion, they will be cancelled. The other party (User or
Speaker) will see your name replaced by a placeholder in past Session
history; the substantive transcript on the **other party's** side is
not removed, in line with the Privacy Policy and the Terms &
Conditions.

## Sub-processors and downstream deletion

Deleting your Gospel Vox account also propagates deletion to our
backend services:

- **Firebase Authentication** — your authentication record is deleted
  immediately, so you can no longer sign in.
- **Cloud Firestore** and **Firebase Storage** — your data is removed
  or anonymised within 30 days.
- **OneSignal** — your push-notification subscription is removed
  within 30 days.
- **Razorpay** — Razorpay retains transaction records under its own
  obligations (RBI rules and Indian tax law); we cannot delete those
  records.

## Reactivating after deletion

Account deletion is permanent. If you sign in again with the same
Google or Apple ID after deletion, a **new** account will be created;
your previous data is not restored.
