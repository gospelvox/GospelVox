Child Safety Standards

Last updated: 4 June 2026

GospelVox ("we", "us", "the Platform") is a Christian spiritual consultation platform connecting adult users with verified clergy and spiritual counsellors ("Priests") for one-to-one voice consultations and written guidance. This page publishes our standards against Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation ("CSAE") in compliance with Google Play's Child Safety Standards Policy for apps in the social and communication categories, the U.S. SHIELD Act of 2018, the Indian Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012, and the IT (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021.

1. Zero-tolerance commitment

GospelVox strictly prohibits any content, behaviour, or activity that sexually exploits or endangers children. This includes, without limitation:

  • Child Sexual Abuse Material ("CSAM") in any form — images, video, audio, written text, drawings, or computer-generated imagery.
  • Grooming, solicitation, or sexual conversation directed at anyone under 18.
  • Sextortion, sexual blackmail, or coercion of minors.
  • Sharing personal information of a minor for the purpose of meeting or contacting them outside the Platform.
  • Promotion, normalisation, or glorification of any of the above.

Violations result in immediate, permanent account termination, removal of all associated content, and reporting to the appropriate law-enforcement and child-protection authorities (see § 6).

2. Minimum-age policy

GospelVox is intended for users aged 18 years and older. Sign-up requires the user to confirm they are at least 18, and our Terms of Service prohibit use by minors. Accounts found to belong to a person under 18 are suspended pending verification; if the user cannot prove they are 18 or over, the account is permanently deleted and all associated data is removed in accordance with our Privacy Policy.

3. In-app reporting

Every user can report a Priest, another user, a chat message, or any content they encounter on the Platform. Reporting tools are available directly from:

  • The three-dot menu on any chat message or session card.
  • The Priest profile page (“Report this Priest”).
  • The session-history screen for any past consultation.
  • Our support email at support@gospelvox.com, with subject line "Child Safety Concern" for priority routing.

Reports are reviewed by our trust-and-safety team within 24 hours. Reports flagged as CSAE-related are escalated immediately and reviewed within 1 hour on a 24×7 rotation.

4. In-app blocking

Users can block any Priest at any time from the Priest profile or the chat screen. Blocking immediately:

  • Stops the blocked party from initiating any new sessions, voice calls, or messages with the user.
  • Hides the blocked party from the user’s feed and search results.
  • Cancels any pending session request between the parties.

Blocking does not notify the blocked party.

5. Content moderation

GospelVox combines automated and human moderation:

  • Automated screening — uploaded profile photos and chat attachments are passed through Google Cloud’s Vision Safe Search API and Firebase ML Kit content filters before they become visible to other users. Anything flagged at a "likely" or "very likely" CSAM probability is blocked at upload and routed to human review.
  • Human moderation — our trust-and-safety team manually reviews every CSAE-flagged report, every newly-onboarded Priest’s profile, and a random sample of session transcripts each week.
  • Hash matching — known CSAM hashes from the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) PhotoDNA database are blocked at upload.
  • Priest KYC — every Priest is verified against government-issued ID and ministry credentials before they can accept paid sessions. Profiles are reviewed manually, not automatically approved.

6. Legal reporting

Where the law requires it, GospelVox reports CSAE incidents to the appropriate authorities:

  • India — reports are filed with the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and the local jurisdictional police under § 19 and § 20 of the POCSO Act, 2012, and the IT Act, 2000.
  • United States — CyberTipline reports are filed with the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) at report.cybertip.org, as required by 18 U.S.C. § 2258A.
  • European Union & United Kingdom — reports are filed with the Internet Watch Foundation (IWF) and the relevant national hotline.
  • Other jurisdictions — we cooperate with INHOPE member hotlines and local law enforcement on request.

We retain identifying records of CSAE incidents for the minimum statutory period required, even where the underlying accounts are deleted, so that authorities can investigate.

8. Designated CSAE point of contact

For child-safety queries from law enforcement, regulators, educators, or parents, please email support@gospelvox.com with subject line "Child Safety Concern". Mails sent with this subject are routed to an on-call moderator and acknowledged within 24 hours.

9. Cooperation with authorities

GospelVox preserves data on request from a lawful authority investigating CSAE for up to 180 days pending a formal request, in line with the U.S. Stored Communications Act and the Indian Code of Criminal Procedure. We honour validly-served preservation orders, subpoenas, and court orders, and produce records consistent with applicable law.

10. Training and policy review

Our moderation team is trained annually on CSAE indicators, grooming patterns, NCMEC and IWF reporting workflows, and trauma-informed handling of victim communications. This Child Safety Standards page is reviewed at least once every 12 months and updated whenever Google Play, Apple, or our applicable statutory framework changes.
